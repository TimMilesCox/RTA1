<h2>Appendix C: Floating Numbers in External Formats</h2>


masmx can also generate floating numbers in single-word, triple-word, five-word,
six-word and eight-word, but RTA1 hardware only uses quad-word for computing
and double-word as a packed format

<p>
One reason for assembling floating constants in sizes which RTA1
instruction processor does not use, but nevertheless in the
RTA1 simple-fraction / midpoint / 1s-complement format, might be
future developments of external graphic or array hardware

<p>
masmx progressive repeat <b>$do</b> assembly may make an important
contribution to ROM floating-constant arrays for external hardware

<p>
Here for example is an assembly of a list of 24-bit floating constants,
intended for some hardware which is not an RTA1 instruction processor
<pre>

$ masmx -ln
MASMX 7r3
0/-INPUT>>
variation	$do	6,	+	1.5s*+19-variation
		$end
00:000000 7DA689              +1 variation $do 6, + 1.5s*+19-variation
00:000001 7A853A              +1 
00:000002 76D52A              +1 
00:000003 73AA88              +1 
00:000004 70886D              +1 
00:000005 6CDA48              +1 
  :                            2   $end
:$(00):000000:000006 


</pre>

and here a list of 192-bit floating constants

<pre>

$ masmx -ln
MASMX 7r3
0/-INPUT>>
explosion_force	$do	5,	+	1.75o*+200000*explosion_force
		$end
00:000000 4A2343AC02108077EA4B351D50490F9155467C8BB63FC339
                              +1 explosion_force $do 5, + 1.75o*+200000*explosion_force
00:000008 544685841574CA296129591B86AE598F504C3310DC4CE197
                              +1 
00:000010 5E69C6CADA3E0E8C111FCC4739971A503FAA6C18027D085A
                              +1 
00:000018 688D089BC4E08B14D8BACDD7FF439E89453D0E2D2EA5677B
                              +1 
00:000020 72B049EF3A5C718A5B04E5FFEF32865C1F3C8CE1D5CABDC1
                              +1 
  :                            2   $end
:$(00):000000:000028 


</pre>

<p>
Non-RTA sized numbers can be assembled especially for external hardware
which is congruent the RTA1 simple-fraction floating layout

<p>
The default characteristic sizes (size of field containing the sign bit and
the mid-point exponent) are
<pre>

	$characteristic,24	8	. 1-word float has 8-bit characteristic and 16-bit mantissa
	$characteristic,72	24	. 3-word float has 24-bit characteristic and 48-bit mantissa
	$characteristic,120	24	. 5-word float has 24-bit characteristic and 96-bit mantissa
	$characteristic,144	24	. 6-word float has 24-bit characteristic and 120-bit mantissa
	$characteristic,192	24	. 8-word float has 24-bit characteristic and 168-bit mantissa

</pre>
<p>
RTA1 instruction processor uses 96-bit and 48-bit floating formats
<p>
48-bit floating constants may not have a characteristic field size
different from the default 12 bits for RTA1 floating processing

<p>
96-bit floating constants may not have a characteristic field size
different from the default 24 bits for RTA1 floating processing

<p>
Few non-RTA instruction processors have the RTA1
simple fraction / midpoint / 1s-complement floating scheme

<p>
This manual is about masmx for RTA1 and not about constant assembly
for machines non-congruent with the 24-bit word and the simple-fraction
floating architecture. masmx Meta-Assembler documentation
discusses in full IEEE754 floating constant generation, but chiefly
in context of assembly target architectures which use IEEE754

<p>
It's readily foreseen that native RTA1 96-bit and 48-bit numbers be
translated dynamically to and from XDR formats

<p>
However XDR and non-RTA1 machines can only represent a microscopic
part of RTA1 floating range. The exchange format for large numbers is
ASCII edit string
